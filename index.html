About

Who I Am

I'm a Computer Science graduate from National University Laguna, with a focus on ML/AI engineering and full-stack web development. I grew up in Los Baños, Laguna — a university town at the foot of Mount Makiling, shaped by UPLB's culture of research, nature, and inquiry.

That same mountain became the dataset source for my undergraduate thesis and the setting for an award-winning game. My work is driven by a desire to automate the inefficiencies I witnessed firsthand in my father's forestry fieldwork — and by a belief that the right technology, applied thoughtfully, can make science more accessible.

🌱 The Seed of Curiosity

It started with a video game. Nier: Automata posed a question I couldn't shake: what actually separates a machine from a human? That question became an obsession, and eventually a compass — pointing me toward artificial intelligence not as a career choice, but as a genuine search for an answer.

👨‍👩‍👦 The Influence of Family

My father, Edwin, is a UPLB-trained forester whose work involves tree inventory — walking through dense forest with a clipboard, manually recording species data. My mother, Mary Grace, is a food scientist whose career was similarly built on meticulous, hands-on fieldwork. I grew up watching people I admired do extraordinary work by hand that I believed machines could help with.

🏞️ The Influence of Environment

Los Baños isn't just where I'm from — it's a place defined by UPLB's culture of scientific inquiry. Growing up surrounded by researchers, laboratories, and the biodiversity of Mount Makiling gave me an intuition that technology and nature aren't opposites. They're collaborators.

⚡ The Catalyst for Change

In high school, I joined my father on a fieldwork survey deep in Makiling's forest reserve. I watched him spend hours recording tree species on paper forms — data that would later be transcribed by hand into spreadsheets. The inefficiency wasn't his fault; it was just how it had always been done. I knew, even then, that there had to be a better way.

🤖 The Journey of Discovery

When ChatGPT launched in late 2022, I recognized it as a turning point — not just a product, but a signal that the AI boom was here. I had been studying computer vision for two years at that point, and suddenly the tools, the talent, and the cultural moment aligned. It was the right time to build something meaningful. My thesis was that something.

🤝 The Power of Collaboration

Leading a 4-member thesis team toward a project that would eventually compete globally taught me that the best systems aren't built alone. The Dipterocarp classifier was more than a model — it was the result of shared curiosity, late nights, and a belief that four CS students from a provincial campus could contribute something real to the world.
